**Participant: Yang Qin, 22 years old, currently a postgraduate student at the University of Queensland**

---

Interviewer: OK, let’s begin. I’ll first introduce our project. This is Deco7180. It is similar to platforms like Dianping, but with some differences. It is designed for Brisbane and specifically targets tourists. 

Its MVP features include combining Google-based ratings and reviews with official government food safety ratings for restaurants. The safety rating ranges from 3 to 5 stars, with 3 being the minimum. We aim to integrate these types of data. 

During our research, we found an issue: some restaurants have very high ratings but very few reviews, while others have many reviews but lower ratings. Different users may value these differently — for example, some may prefer places with more reviews regardless of rating. So we introduced a **weight adjustment feature** to control how results are ranked.

Another feature is the **custom route function**. Since this is for tourists, users can draw their own route on the map and explore restaurants within that area.

---

Interviewer: If you were visiting here for the first time, which part of this platform would you value the most?

Yang Qin: Probably the feature that lets you plan your route and also recommends nearby restaurants at the same time. As a tourist, you don’t want to waste time, so finding restaurants along your planned route is very useful.

For example, when traveling in Europe, we usually arrive at a place, like our accommodation, and then look for highly rated restaurants nearby. Sometimes we also go specifically for certain types of food, like a steakhouse. So restaurants can become part of the travel plan.

---

Interviewer: What do you think about the filter and weight-adjustment features? Would you use them?

Yang Qin: The safety rating could be somewhat useful as a reference. But in extreme cases — like very high ratings with very few reviews, or many reviews with low ratings — I usually wouldn’t rely on that as my main decision factor.

I think standard sorting options like “most relevant” or “most popular” are already sufficient. These weight-adjustment features don’t feel very necessary to me.

Instead, maybe you could add something like a **user tagging or marking system**. If many users flag a restaurant, it could show a warning or highlight — like telling users to be cautious. That might be more useful than adjusting weights.

---

Interviewer: You mentioned earlier that the custom route feature is interesting. Do you think it is helpful?

Yang Qin: Yes, I think it’s quite useful and interesting.

---

Interviewer: When choosing a restaurant, what do you personally care about the most — safety, taste, or popularity?

Yang Qin: Definitely taste. Taste is the most important factor for me.

Also, I noticed you haven’t included food categories yet — like Chinese food, steak, etc. That’s actually very important.

For example, if I want Chinese food or steak today, I would definitely use category filtering first. It’s one of the most important features.

Your original idea of drawing a route and then finding restaurants nearby is good, but if there are no restaurants of the type I want, then I wouldn’t go anyway. So category filtering is essential.
