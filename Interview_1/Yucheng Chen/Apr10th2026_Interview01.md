**Participant: Bo Wang, 30 years old, currently a postgraduate student at the University of Queensland**

---

Interviewer: Alright, let’s begin. This interview is about our DECO7180 prototype. I’ll first give you a brief introduction. This system is designed specifically for tourists to help them find restaurants they want to visit — that’s the core function. 

Our MVP combines two types of data: government-provided food safety ratings, and user-generated data such as number of reviews and star ratings from online platforms. One is official data, and the other reflects public opinion. These are combined and presented to users to help them choose restaurants and view rankings. 

Another feature is the custom route function, where users can create their own route and see recommended restaurants along the way. There is also a weight-adjustment feature, allowing users to adjust the importance of ratings, review counts, and safety scores, since some restaurants have high ratings but few reviews, while others have many reviews but lower ratings. Users can filter based on their preferences, including safety level.

---

Interviewer: Imagine you are visiting this city for the first time. When you see this prototype, which features feel most important to you? Which ones feel less important?

Bo Wang: I think if I’m traveling to a city, I might first expect recommendations for attractions. But if this is purely a restaurant-focused app, then the route feature becomes the most important. For example, if I’m going from one place to another, I would want to see restaurants along that route. 

The weight-adjustment feature depends on the user. Some people might explore food as part of experiencing the city — like visiting local snacks along a route. In that case, it would be useful to plan routes based on local specialties. But I think the route should be user-defined rather than system-recommended. It should reflect personal preferences.

---

Interviewer: From your perspective, if you were in a new city, would this app be attractive to you?

Bo Wang: It could be, but I think food categories are more important than just showing restaurants along a route. For example, if I go to the Gold Coast, I might specifically want seafood. So I would prefer filtering by categories like seafood, Japanese food, buffet, desserts, or drinks.

When I travel, I usually have a clear idea of what I want to eat. I wouldn’t just explore randomly. Even if a restaurant has a high rating, if it’s not the type of food I want, I wouldn’t choose it. So category filtering is very important.

---

Interviewer: What do you think about the weight-adjustment feature?

Bo Wang: For me, I care more about the number of reviews, so I might adjust the weight toward that. But there’s a problem — some restaurants have many reviews but low ratings. So relying only on review count isn’t ideal.

I would prefer something like first filtering by rating (for example, 3.5 to 5 stars), and then sorting by review count. That feels more practical.

Also, it’s hard to define weights precisely. I think this kind of decision is often intuitive. For example, a 5-star restaurant with only 100 reviews versus one with tens of thousands — I might choose the one with more reviews if the positive rate is high.

Maybe something like positive review percentage would be more useful. Also, price might be an important factor, since some highly rated restaurants have fewer reviews because they are expensive.

---

Interviewer: As a tourist, would you care about food safety ratings?

Bo Wang: Not really. In countries like Australia, most restaurants already have licenses, so I assume they meet a basic standard. Brisbane uses a star system from 3 to 5 stars, and 3 stars is still acceptable.

Even if it’s 3 stars, I probably wouldn’t pay much attention. I grew up eating street food, so I’m less sensitive to that. Usually, if I want to judge hygiene, I would look at user reviews instead.

That said, official safety ratings could still be helpful as a reference. But I wouldn’t actively look for them. If they are clearly displayed on the homepage, like with icons or colors, that might help.

---

Interviewer: Do you see any concerns with how safety ratings are displayed?

Bo Wang: Yes, there might be an ethical issue. For example, if 3 stars is shown in red, it might make it look unsafe, even though it’s still acceptable. That could negatively affect businesses. So the use of color needs to be considered carefully.

---

Interviewer: Do you think the custom route feature is useful?

Bo Wang: Yes, I think it can definitely be helpful in some situations.

---

Interviewer: Finally, what do you think about the overall interface and logic?

Bo Wang: I think it makes sense. The main interface is a map, with filters on one side. The map updates in real time based on filters and weight adjustments. Search and login are on the right.

The custom route function, similar to Google Maps, is also clear. In the future, if users can draw routes directly on the map and see recommended restaurants along the way, that would be very useful.

Also, if the system can include user preferences, like preferring Western food or desserts, and highlight those along the route, that would improve the experience.

Overall, I think category filtering is very important, because it helps users quickly find what they actually want.
