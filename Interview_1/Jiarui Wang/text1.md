# Interview Transcript

## Participant 1

**Interviewer:** Hello, may I ask whether it is okay to record this interview? You can pause or ask me to delete it at any time.

**Participant:** Yes.

**Interviewer:** Our team has created a low-fidelity prototype of a website. First question: if this platform were fully developed, would you actively use the **food safety filter** when searching for a restaurant?

**Participant:** Yes, I would. After all, I care a lot about safety when eating out. If I get sick after dining, it would be a big problem.

**Interviewer:** Why would this be important to you?

**Participant:** Because food safety directly affects my health, so it would be one of the things I check before making a decision.

**Interviewer:** Does the concept of **finding restaurants along a planned route** match your actual travel habits?

**Participant:** Yes, it does.

**Interviewer:** Can you give an example of when you would prefer this instead of just searching for “restaurants near me”?

**Participant:** If my travel schedule is tight and I have many places to visit, I would prefer to plan my dining options along the route in advance.

**Interviewer:** If a restaurant already has good user reviews, would the added **official food safety rating** change your mind?

**Participant:** Yes, to some extent.

**Interviewer:** How low would the food safety rating need to be for you to consider cancelling a booking?

**Participant:** If the food safety rating were below **3.5**, I would consider cancelling the booking.

**Interviewer:** Would you trust a map platform that combines **official government hygiene data** with normal restaurant search functions?

**Participant:** If the platform could clearly show the source of the data and prove that it is authentic, then I would trust it.

**Interviewer:** Based on the wireframes you just viewed, do you think the sequence of **searching, filtering, and route planning** feels logical? Is anything missing?

**Participant:** I think the process is logical. At the moment, I cannot think of any missing steps.

**Interviewer:** Compared with platforms like Google Maps, what do you think is the biggest potential advantage or disadvantage of using this kind of **safe food routing map**?

**Participant:** One possible disadvantage is that it may not be able to compete with Google Maps in terms of the number of restaurants available and the amount of detailed information provided. But the advantage is that it makes route-based meal planning easier.

**Interviewer:** Imagine the platform recommends a restaurant on your route. It has a **5-star taste rating** from users, but only a **2-star hygiene rating** from the government. How would you make your final decision?

**Participant:** As I said before, if the hygiene rating is below a certain standard, I would exclude it first. Taste is important, but health and safety come first.

**Interviewer:** Since this is only a basic draft, what two pieces of information **must** be included in the final version for you to actually book a table through it?

**Participant:** The restaurant’s **opening hours** and the **menu**. I also think it would be useful to know the **number of available seats during the current time period**.

**Interviewer:** Thank you for participating in our interview.

**Participant:** You’re welcome.
