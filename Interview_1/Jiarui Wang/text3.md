# Interview Transcript

## Participant 2 – Alternative Version

**Interviewer:** Thank you for participating in our interview. During the interview, you can ask me to pause, skip questions, or delete this recording at any time. Do you allow us to record this interview?

**Participant:** Yes.

**Interviewer:** Thank you. This is the low-fidelity prototype we created. First question: if this platform were fully developed, would you use the **food safety filter** when looking for a restaurant?

**Participant:** Yes, I would definitely use it.

**Interviewer:** Why is this important to you?

**Participant:** Because when I choose a restaurant, food safety is a basic requirement, not just an extra feature. Especially in an unfamiliar area, I want a quick way to avoid places that may have hygiene risks, so this filter would save me time and give me more confidence in my decision.

**Interviewer:** Does the concept of **looking for restaurants along a planned route** match your actual travel habits?

**Participant:** Yes, it does.

**Interviewer:** Can you give an example of when you would prefer this instead of just searching for restaurants nearby?

**Participant:** For example, if I am travelling from my hotel to a transport station or from one attraction to another, I would rather choose a restaurant that fits naturally into my route. That way I do not need to make a detour just for food, and it makes the whole trip more efficient.

**Interviewer:** If a restaurant already has good user reviews, would the added **official food safety rating** change your opinion?

**Participant:** Yes, it would still affect my decision.

**Interviewer:** Why?

**Participant:** Because user reviews usually focus more on taste, service, or atmosphere, while food safety is about whether the place meets a minimum health standard. Even if a restaurant is popular, I would still take the hygiene score seriously.

**Interviewer:** How low would the food safety rating have to be for you to cancel a booking?

**Participant:** If it were below **3 stars**, I would probably cancel the booking. For me, that would already be a warning sign.

**Interviewer:** Would you trust a platform that combines **official government hygiene data** with normal restaurant search functions?

**Participant:** Yes, I would be willing to trust it.

**Interviewer:** What would the platform need to show to prove the data is authentic?

**Participant:** It should clearly show where the data comes from, when it was last updated, and ideally provide a link to the official inspection report. If the source is transparent and the information is current, I would feel much more comfortable relying on it.

**Interviewer:** Based on the basic wireframes you just viewed, do you think the steps from **searching, filtering, and route planning** are logical? Is anything missing in the flow?

**Participant:** Yes, the process feels logical overall.

**Interviewer:** Is there anything you would like to add?

**Participant:** I think it would be even better if the platform also included **average cost**, **real-time crowd level or waiting time**, and **user reviews**. These details would make it easier to compare options and decide more quickly.

**Interviewer:** Compared with the way you currently search for restaurants, such as using Google Maps, what do you think is the biggest potential advantage or disadvantage of using this kind of **safe food routing map**?

**Participant:** The biggest advantage is that it combines route planning with food safety, which makes it more focused than a general map platform. The disadvantage is that, at least in the beginning, it may not have as many restaurants or as much detailed information as Google Maps.

**Interviewer:** Imagine the platform recommends a restaurant on your route. It has a **5-star taste rating** from users, but only a **2-star hygiene rating** from the government. How would you make your final decision?

**Participant:** I would not choose it. A high taste rating might make it attractive, but a 2-star hygiene rating would be too risky for me. I would rather pick a safer option, even if it is slightly less popular.

**Interviewer:** Since this is only a basic draft, if you really wanted to book a table through this platform, what two key pieces of information **must** be included in the final version?

**Participant:** First, it must show a reliable and up-to-date **food safety rating** with some inspection details. Second, it should include practical booking information such as **business hours**, **seat availability**, **average spending**, and **contact details**. Without these, I would probably still switch to another platform before making a booking.

**Interviewer:** Thank you for participating in our interview. I will stop the recording now.

**Participant:** Okay.
