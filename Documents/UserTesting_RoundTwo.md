# User Testing Analysis: Round Two

## 1. Trust and Credibility Analysis

Initial Attraction: High-quality food photos (65.2%) and user ratings (65.2%) are the primary factors that first attract user attention and influence initial judgment.

Safety vs. Ratings: If a restaurant has high ratings but a low food safety score, 47.8% of users would hesitate and check the official report, while 30.4% would avoid it entirely.

Platform Trust: 95.7% of users agree that the "Verify Official Report" feature increases their trust in the platform.

Critical Missing Info: Users identified "Full menu images" (56.5%) as the most important missing information needed to make a final decision.

---

## 2. Route Planning and Optimization

User Workflow Preference: 95.7% of users prefer starting with a system-generated route and then adjusting it, rather than planning from scratch.

Primary Optimization Metric: "Total estimated time" (39.1%) is considered the most useful information when optimizing a route.

Editing Efficiency: 52.2% of users find that editing a suggested route moderately reduces their planning effort.

Real-time Feedback Needs: When adjusting a route, users find "Updated total walking time" (60.9%) and "Updated total waiting time" (56.5%) to be the most helpful feedback.

---

## 3. Modification Triggers

Users cited the following as primary reasons for modifying a recommended route:

Walking Distance: 52.2% modify routes if the walking distance is too far.

Wishlist Integration: 43.5% want to include restaurants from their personal wishlist.

Waiting Time: 43.5% modify routes if the waiting time is too long.

Category Mismatch: 39.1% adjust routes when the food category does not match their preference.

---

## 4. Interface and User Experience

Cognitive Load: 43.5% of users feel neutral about the amount of information on the restaurant card, though 17.4% find it somewhat cluttered.

Filter Usability: 56.5% of users find the category filters easy to find and use.

Social Context: 60.9% of users are neutral regarding the "Solo Mode / Group Mode" feature, suggesting it is not currently a high-priority differentiator.
